# subedit

Full sub-edit pipeline: evaluate voice and AI tells, produce problem list, author approval gate, apply fixes, checklist, deliver. This is an end-to-end editorial workflow for line editing (voice polish, gentle compression) and micro editing (typos, grammar, continuity only). Use this when the scene works structurally but the prose needs cleaning.

## Fortes

| Step | Forte | Mode | Receives additionally |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 (parallel) | voice-guardian | evaluate | — |
| 1 (parallel) | cognition-sensor | evaluate | — |
| 2 | prose-scribe | revision | Approved fixes |

## Coordination pattern

Three-phase pipeline with approval gate.

**Phase 1 — Evaluate.** Voice-guardian and cognition-sensor fire in parallel. Voice-guardian applies three silent reads, problem list (max 15), red flag test, never-change rules, and lens check. Cognition-sensor applies the kill list and both lenses (Another Consciousness, Costly Cognition).

**Phase 2 — Problem list and approve.** The orchestrator compiles findings into a problem list. Each item: exact quote, issue, proposed fix or "PRESERVE + flag." The author approves, modifies, or rejects each fix individually.

**Phase 3 — Fix and verify.** Prose-scribe applies approved fixes with minimum alteration. Change log and pre-submission checklist delivered.

## Context requirements

Before evaluation fortes fire:

- Scene/chapter content (from Scrivener via scrivener-nav)
- Each forte's spec and all talents from its "Draws on" section
- Prose-scribe spec and talents (for phase 3)

## Procedure

1. **Resolve scope.** Scene or chapter. Parse the Scrivener binder.

2. **Check maturity.** Sub-editing at To Do (1) or First Draft (2) is premature — the prose is still settling. Report this and ask whether to proceed. At Second Draft (28), proceed.

3. **Fire voice-guardian and cognition-sensor in parallel (Phase 1).** Both default to PRESERVE when uncertain. Voice-guardian checks: voice markers (positive and negative), character voice signatures, the kill list, the 12 never-change rules, smart quotes, Australian spelling. Cognition-sensor checks: kill list items, deferral patterns, nominalisation, both lenses.

4. **Compile problem list (Phase 2).** Merge findings. For each item: exact quote, one-sentence issue, minimal fix, "necessary not better" filter. Separate craft-level findings from surface corrections (typos, spacing, punctuation). Present individually to the author.

5. **Author approval gate.** Each fix: approve, modify, or reject. Uncertain items flagged as PRESERVE + author decision.

6. **Fire prose-scribe in revision mode (Phase 3).** Apply only approved fixes. Minimum alteration. Zero additions. Smart quotes verified. Change log for every alteration.

7. **Pre-submission checklist.** All quotes smart. Only approved changes made. No unapproved additions or compressions. Word count within 5% (unless approved). Australian spelling intact. Tax/taxing preserved. Change log complete. Every change defensible as necessary. Any box unchecked: do not present as complete.

8. **Deliver.** Revised prose, change log, checklist.

## Output

- Evaluation files from voice-guardian and cognition-sensor
- Problem list with author decisions
- Revised prose with change log
- Pre-submission checklist

## Settlement condition

All approved fixes applied. Checklist passes. Change log is complete.

## Boundary

Line editing and micro editing. For structural editing (pacing, character, scene function), use `/developmental`. The sub-edit operates on prose that already works as storytelling — it cleans, it does not restructure.
