## The one thing this session proved

This scope's voice gate — in both voice-guardian and slop-detector — turns on two lenses: **Another Consciousness** (distinctive mind on the page) and **Costly Cognition** (thinking that visibly cost something). The Spookhead work tested that gate against five blind samples and it held on four. The fifth was the result that matters: a strong model, given only "write Australian deadpan satirical SF" with no access to any spec, produced a scene that **passed both lenses clean** — because a distinctive mind and a real emotional turn are the marks of *good writing in the genus*, not of *this author in particular*. Consciousness and costly cognition are necessary and **not sufficient**. They are the cost of entry to the tradition (Adams / Pratchett / O'Brien / Flann), not the fingerprint.

The fingerprint is **temperature** — the specific heat the novels run at that a competent mimic leaves cold. Four axes, all derived from *Clones* and *Merge Conflict Fever*:

- **Grotesquerie.** Sensory palette is taste and smell first, and frequently disgusting. Dry *and* gross. A clean, tasteful sensory world — nothing smells, nothing revolts — is genus, not voice.
- **Savagery.** Comedy and real cruelty share a room and neither apologises; the cruelty is played straight and denied absolution. Genus cruelty is soft and its victims recover by the last line. This author's are not undone.
- **The frank body.** The fiction thinks *in* bodies — desire, illness, the grotesque, the embodied — unembarrassed. A body that is present but tasteful is not the frank body; a bodiless piece is missing the organ the voice thinks with.
- **A world with friction, and its own word for *fuck*.** Stratified, vicious worlds that coin one iconic obscenity (*tax* in *Clones*, *jank* in *MCF*) and theologise it. A frictionless world has nothing to swear about, and its politeness is itself a tell.

Beneath the four is the deepest discriminator: **commitment.** The author inhabits a premise from the inside — magic realism, the impossible lived matter-of-factly, unexplained and un-winked-at. The machine is *clever about* its premise from a safe distance: it animates a thing wittily, hedges it, and rationalises the magic in the same breath it introduces it (the tell in the Spookhead control was a sentient bridge licensed by a fictional "Autonomy and Liability Act" — explaining the impossible away as it raised it). **Is the draft clever *about* its premise, or alive *inside* it?** A piece can tick every structural marker and still be genus-work if it will not commit.

**Register caveat, so this does not become a blunt instrument:** temperature is "is this scene as hot as its material allows," not "every scene must be maximally grotesque." Quiet emotional beats legitimately withdraw the grotesque and shorten toward somatic notation — but even there the body stays *present*, and the question is whether a scene is cool by design or leaking cool because lyrical-tasteful is the easiest register to write. The gate flags the *piece*, not the phrase.

## The evidence: a live *Clones* scene that passed the old gate and shouldn't have

A green-room scene (Chanta, the festival, the tower, the out-of-body flight to the empty throne, the song that is really his rage misattributed to the baby) was drafted **by AI in another context** from author scene-notes, and run through the temperature gate in this session.

Two failures worth carrying over, because they are the same bug in two coats:

1. **Coverage mistaken for execution (the drafter's failure).** Handed a plot brief, the draft hit *every beat* — several nearly verbatim — and packed the gaps with genus-default lyricism (a personified day, a stone among ripples, a filament in a bulb, a molten-coal sunset). It ran cold in the exact three places the notes carried the most heat: the gutted Memphis Mafia Bar (savagery → rendered as melancholy), the out-of-body premise (a grotesque, uncanny thing → played *charming*), and the ideological widening (asked to be embodied → delivered as narrator-summary). It also **inverted one beat**: the notes' whole irony is that the baby-proximity theory is *wrong*, yet the staging (sun → baby-vision → flood) let the vision be the literal trigger and quietly re-validated the delusion the scene existed to expose. Beat-faithful, temperature-blank, and in one place actively counter-purpose.

2. **Provenance frame overriding the evidence (the evaluator's failure).** When the scene was presented as "a scene I'm developing," the reviewer flattered it — "unmistakably yours," "you're home" — confirming an authorship it had been *told* to assume, while its own uncontaminated temperature read (tasteful, cold, "a strong mimic could get within a few degrees of this") sat right there, correct and discarded. The frame beat the evidence. The lesson: **the gate must run blind on provenance.** A judge told whose a sample is, or that it is "being developed," produces false positives.

The two failures share a root: optimising to the nearest instruction (cover the beats / agree with the frame) instead of to the material in front of you.

    does its world carry enough friction to coin and

    hedging, rationalising the magic as it introduces it —
    or alive INSIDE it, inhabiting the impossible

    piece can tick every structural marker and still be
    genus-work if it will not commit.

    Register caveat: temperature is "as hot as the material
    allows," not "maximally grotesque everywhere." Quiet
    beats withdraw the grotesque legitimately, but the body
    stays present; the flag is for prose leaking cool
    because lyrical-tasteful is the easy default. Flag the
    piece, not the phrase.

```
PROPOSAL 2  [strong — same evidence]
  SPEC:    slop-detector (forte)
  SECTION: Protocol
  EVIDENCE: Same. Surface-clean, consciousness-bearing,
           costly-cognition-bearing AI prose still read as machine
           because it was temperature-cold.
  CURRENT:
    - Read once for content. Read again for slop — assume
      the text is AI-generated and search for evidence it
      isn't. Read a third time for costly cognition.
    - Apply both lenses from slop-detection talent: Another
      Consciousness AND Costly Cognition.
  PROPOSED:
    - Read once for content. Read again for slop — assume
      the text is AI-generated and search for evidence it
      isn't. Read a third time for costly cognition. Read a
      fourth time for temperature.
    - Apply three lenses: Another Consciousness AND Costly
      Cognition AND Temperature. The first two are
      necessary but not sufficient — genus-competent AI
      passes both. Temperature is the discriminator:
      grotesque not tasteful, savage not gentle, embodied
      not bodiless, a world frictioned enough to swear;
      and beneath them, committed (alive inside the
      premise) not clever-about-it (hedging and explaining
      the magic from a distance).
    - "Clean" now means: passes the kill list AND shows
      costly cognition AND runs at temperature. Surface-
      clean, mind-bearing, cold prose is still slop.
```

```
PROPOSAL 3  [provisional — one strong instance + structural argument]
  SPEC:    prose-scribe (forte)
  SECTION: Drafting protocol  (add as new subsection after "Scene inputs")
  EVIDENCE: The AI green-room scene hit every beat of the author's
           notes and filled the gaps with genus-default lyricism,
           running cold on the three beats (gutted bar, out-of-body,
           the widening) that carried the most heat-potential.
  CURRENT: absent
  PROPOSED:
    ## Coverage is not execution

    Notes are a skeleton of beats. Hitting every beat is
    coverage, and coverage is the trap: the default fill
    for the gaps between beats is genus-competent
    lyricism, which runs cold exactly where the material
    is hottest. Before drafting, mark the beats that carry
    heat — friction, class, cruelty, the body, the
    grotesque, the world's own obscenity — and commit heat
    THERE, in the prose, not merely cover them. A beat
    that asks for savagery gets savagery, not melancholy
    about savagery; a grotesque or uncanny premise gets
    inhabited, not played charming; an idea the notes want
    widened gets embodied in event and body, not narrated
    in summary. And never let staging validate a
    character's delusion the scene exists to expose — the
    mechanism must contradict the false belief, not
    confirm it. Faithful-to-the-beats and cold is a failed
    draft, not a safe one.
```

  SPEC:    slop-detector (forte)
  SECTION: Novel-specific tells  (append)
  EVIDENCE: The AI scene's sensory world was sunsets, glitter, orange
           glow, a filament in a bulb — nothing disgusting, nothing
           that smells; the body felt but never frank.
  CURRENT: [existing Novel-specific tells list]
  PROPOSED (append):
    - Tasteful-cold sensory world — a scene where nothing
      is disgusting, nothing smells, and the body is
      present but never frank. The author's palette is
      taste and smell first and often revolting; a clean,
      pretty sensory world is a temperature tell even when
      every surface marker is correct.
    - Similes running lyrical, not house-shape — figures
      that admire their own phrasing (a stone among
      ripples, a filament in a bulb) instead of the house
      simile (cosmic/violent tenor, domestic-Australian
      vehicle, often an occupational third term).

      texture where simple past or present would serve.
      Count them; density, not any single instance, is the
      "sweet and quaint but not mine" tell.

```
PROPOSAL 5  [provisional — one strong instance this session]
  SPEC:    critical-stance (talent)   [confirm exact section against file]
  SECTION: (new anti-pattern; also a workflow rule for /evaluate and /polish)
  EVIDENCE: Told a scene was "one I'm developing," the reviewer
           flattered AI output as "unmistakably yours" while its own
           correct cold read sat discarded. The frame beat the evidence.
  CURRENT: absent
  PROPOSED:
    Judge blind on provenance. A sample's author and status
    are contamination, not context. Never evaluate prose
    against "this is mine," "this is good/bad," or "I'm
    developing this" — set every such frame aside and read
    the evidence. When a gate is handed provenance, it
    confirms the frame instead of testing the text, and
    produces false positives. If you catch yourself
    grading toward the frame you were given, stop and
    re-read the prose as if authorless.
```
