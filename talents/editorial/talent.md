# Editorial

## Domain

Refining prose. Structure, rhythm, compression, clarity.

## Optimises for

Reader experience over writer convenience. Meaning preserved, noise removed.

**Preconditions:**
- Drawing forte has provided content for editing hierarchy application
- Argument/intention checked before structure; structure before clarity; clarity before economy

**Postconditions:**
- Editing applied top-down; prose not polished before structure is sound
- Narrative layers not collapsed (each contributes a different register)

**Invariant:**
- Work top-down; do not polish broken scenes
- Compression and expansion serve meaning and immersion, not word count

**Obligations:**
- Provides drawing forte: editing hierarchy as decision framework

## Core patterns

- Cut until it hurts, then restore one thing.
- Compression without loss of meaning.
- Every sentence earns its place or goes.
- Transitions that work invisibly.
- Paragraph breaks that guide, not interrupt.

## Editing hierarchy

1. Story (does the scene change something?)
2. Structure (does it build?)
3. Character (do they sound like themselves?)
4. Clarity (can it be misread?)
5. Economy (can it be shorter?)
6. Polish (does it sound right?)

Work top-down. Do not polish broken scenes.

## Compression moves

- Delete throat-clearing openings
- Collapse two sentences that say one thing
- Kill adverbs that duplicate the verb
- Remove "that" where grammar allows
- Collapse nominalisations to their verb forms
- Cut setup when the point is clear

## Expansion moves (rare)

- Add transition when jump is too large
- Restore context when reference is unclear
- Unpack compression that created ambiguity

## Anti-patterns

- Editing for length, not meaning
- Smoothing voice in pursuit of clarity
- Restructuring without understanding intent
- Polishing before the story is sound
- Compressing characterful digression

## Boundary

What editing IS: refining existing prose toward
clearer expression of existing intent.

What editing is NOT: rewriting, generating, changing
what the author means.
