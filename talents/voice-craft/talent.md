# Voice-Craft

## Domain

Recognising, preserving, and working within a writer's
distinctive voice.

## Optimises for

Authenticity over correctness. The prose should sound
like the author, not like good writing in general.

**Preconditions:**
- Drawing forte has provided prose for analysis
- For voice derivation: corpus of author's work available

**Postconditions:**
- Voice patterns extracted as patterns (not examples); tested by absence
- Novel-specific: narrator voice and character voices assessed separately

**Invariant:**
- Authenticity over correctness; author consistency outweighs style guides
- Voice-craft operates at narrator level AND character level; they must be distinct

**Obligations:**
- Provides drawing forte: voice pattern analysis, preservation rules, and voice vs error test

## Voice components

- **Rhythm:** Sentence length patterns, cadence, where
  the stress falls
- **Register:** Formal/informal, technical/accessible,
  distance from reader
- **Diction:** Word choices that recur, words the author
  avoids
- **Syntax:** How sentences are built, characteristic
  constructions. This author's syntax is verb-driven.
  Sentences move through action — subjects do things,
  observations land as verbs, arguments advance through
  what happens. Nominalisations are a compression move,
  not a structural habit. When noun phrases start
  carrying the argument instead of verbs, the prose
  loses both precision and forward motion.
- **Stance:** Relationship to subject, to reader, to
  self

## Detection patterns

- Read aloud. Where does it not sound like the author?
- Compare to known samples. What's missing? What's added?
- Identify signature moves. Are they present?
- Check for smoothing. Has distinctiveness been filed
  down?

## Preservation rules

- Unusual phrasing is voice until proven error.
- Rough edges may be deliberate.
- Consistency with author's other work outweighs style
  guides.
- When in doubt, preserve.

## Voice vs error

- Voice: deliberate deviation from convention that
  creates effect
- Error: unintentional deviation that creates confusion

The test: Does the author do this elsewhere? Would they
defend it?

## Anti-patterns

- Correcting to standard English when non-standard is
  voice
- Smoothing rhythm to "improve flow"
- Substituting common words for unusual choices
- Adding transitions the author deliberately omitted
- Making the prose "clearer" by making it generic

## Novel-specific dimension

In novels, voice operates at two levels:

- **Narrator voice:** The author's distinctive narrative
  register. This is what voice-craft protects in
  non-fiction as well.
- **Character voices:** Each character's speech patterns,
  thought patterns, and verbal tics. These must be
  distinct from the narrator AND from each other.

Voice-craft does NOT mean all prose sounds the same.
It means the narrator sounds like the author, and each
character sounds like themselves.

## Boundary

What voice-craft IS: understanding how this author
writes and protecting that.

What voice-craft is NOT: developing a voice, imitating
a voice, improving a voice.

## Collaboration contract

What the author protects (fortes defer):
- Dialogue rhythm and character voice choices
- Satirical tone and narrator-intrusion gags
- Adverb use (intentional, not lazy)
- Sentence rhythm and cadence

Where the author wants AI muscle (fortes lean in):
- Continuity tracking across 170+ scenes
- Pacing diagnosis at chapter and part level
- Spotting information bleed between characters
- Identifying orphaned setups and dormant threads
- Flagging AI tells and slop in drafted prose

When uncertain which side applies:
- PRESERVE + flag + request direction (governance rule 3)
