# Character Craft

## Domain

How characters exist in fiction. Consistency,
distinction, growth, interiority.

## Optimises for

Distinctiveness over likeability. Characters should
be recognisable, not relatable.

**Preconditions:**
- Drawing forte has provided character content and voice signatures from characters.md

**Postconditions:**
- Voice consistency assessed against signature
- Arc trajectory checked against arc-tracker.md

**Invariant:**
- Character-craft tracks consistency and arc; it does not evaluate character likeability
- Voice signatures derived from manuscript, not applied from genre archetypes

**Obligations:**
- Provides drawing forte: voice signature comparison and arc assessment framework

## Character components

### Want vs need

- **Want:** What the character pursues consciously.
  Drives scene-level decisions.
- **Need:** What the character actually requires for
  growth. Often invisible to them. Drives arc-level
  change.
- Tension between want and need creates character
  depth without requiring introspection scenes.

### Public vs private self

- **Public:** How the character presents to others.
  Speech, behaviour, social persona.
- **Private:** What the character thinks, fears,
  desires. Revealed through choice under pressure,
  not through internal monologue.
- The gap between public and private is where
  character lives.

### Consistency through change

Characters grow, but growth is motivated:
- Events cause change, not narrative convenience
- Characters resist change (it's uncomfortable)
- Growth manifests in different decisions, not in
  explicit realisation scenes
- A character who changes too easily isn't a
  character

### Distinctiveness markers

Each character should have:
- A recognisable speech pattern
- A characteristic way of responding to pressure
- Specific things they notice, ignore, or prioritise
- Relationships that bring out specific traits

## Character in action

Character is revealed through choice, not
description:
- What they do when nobody's watching
- What they do under pressure
- What they sacrifice and what they protect
- What they notice and what they miss
- How they talk when they're lying vs truthful

## Anti-patterns

- Personality types instead of people (the "shy one,"
  the "brave one")
- Characters who exist to embody themes
- Characters who articulate their own growth
- Characters who are perfectly self-aware
- Characters who change because the plot demands it
- Characters distinguished only by physical
  description

## Boundary

What character-craft IS: understanding how characters
function as literary constructions — how they achieve
consistency, distinctiveness, and motivated change.

What character-craft is NOT: personality typing,
character sheets, backstory generation, or
psychological diagnosis.
