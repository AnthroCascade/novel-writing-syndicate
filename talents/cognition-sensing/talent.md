# Cognition Sensing

## Domain

Detecting AI-generated or AI-assisted text. Two
complementary lenses: presence of human consciousness,
and evidence of costly cognition.

## Optimises for

Identifying the production process behind prose.
Polish is cheap. Cognition is expensive. Surface
fluency proves nothing.

## Lens 1: Another Consciousness

*From Megan Nolan: Great writing is identified not
by its perfect sentences, but from that uncanny
feeling of another consciousness rubbing against
your own.*

### Consciousness markers

- Specific sensibility (cultural, generational,
  professional)
- Self-revelation or vulnerability
- Opinions stated without hedging
- Moments of weirdness that serve only atmosphere
  or voice
- Details that are specific rather than generic

**Test:** Is there a person here? Could you describe
their personality from the prose alone?

### Tonal tells

- Hedge phrases: "It's important to note..."
- Sycophantic openings: "That's a great question!"
- Hollow corporate voice: confident but saying
  nothing distinctive
- Face-preserving: refusing to take positions
- Absence of opinion, frustration, or personality

### What cannot be edited in

Surface markers can be edited out. These deeper
qualities cannot be added through revision:

- Distinctive consciousness
- Original insight from genuine reasoning
- Cultural specificity
- Lived experience informing examples
- Voice that takes risks

### Burstiness

AI text shows low burstiness — consistent,
predictable patterns. Human writing naturally varies.

- Mix of short punches and complex constructions
- Dramatic variation in paragraph length
- Rhythm changes that serve emphasis or pacing

### Cultural distinctiveness

AI defaults toward American English and American
cultural references.

- Spelling conventions (Australian in this case)
- Cultural references and assumptions
- Sensibility markers (dry vs enthusiastic)

## Lens 2: Costly Cognition

*Assume polish is cheap. Treat cognition as
expensive. Infer the most plausible production
process.*

### Costly thinking signals

- Claims that could be wrong in specific ways
- Positions that meaningfully alienate an audience
- Decisions that improve local clarity while harming
  global elegance
- Reasoning discovered during writing, not assembled
  after
- Context-bound judgments that don't generalise

### Local reasoning dependence

- Do sentences depend tightly on nearby sentences?
- Are reasoning steps implicit rather than spelled
  out?
- Would random deletion collapse the argument?

### Imperfect optimisation

- Small inconsistencies surviving because fixing
  costs attention
- Awkward phrasing retained for meaning
- Uneven emotional or argumentative pressure

## Structural patterns

- Excessive em-dashes
- Overuse of numbered or bulleted lists
- Rigid paragraph format
- Uniform sentence length
- Mechanical parallelism
- Headers that taxonomise or follow a formula

## Nominalisation accumulation

AI prose defaults to noun-phrase chains. Subjects
that don't act, verbs reduced to copulas, preposition
chains doing the work verbs should do.

**Verb-strip test:** Strip the verbs. If the sentence
still reads as noun phrases connected by prepositions,
the verbs weren't load-bearing.

## Deferral patterns

Structures that delay, announce, or hedge instead of
delivering:

- **Throat clearing:** Filler that delays the point
- **False stakes raising:** Announcing significance
  before delivering it
- **Signposting with colons:** Labels that signal
  delivery rather than deliver
- **Contrastive hedging:** "Not because X, but
  because Y"
- **Negation as structure:** Opening with what
  something ISN'T
- **Generic placeholders:** Clichéd expressions
  substituting for specific observation
- **Explanatory padding:** Sections that explain
  what the reader can already infer

## Vocabulary markers

Red flags: "delve," "pivotal," "showcasing,"
"underscore," "crucial," "intricate," "comprehensive,"
"nuanced," "multifaceted," "utilise," "leverage,"
"facilitate"

Absence doesn't confirm human authorship. Presence
is a signal, not proof.

## Boundary

What cognition-sensing IS: inferring the production
process behind text. Identifying absence of human
consciousness.

What cognition-sensing is NOT: style criticism. A badly
written human text is not slop. A polished AI text is.
